Edsger W. Dijkstra

Computer Programming Pioneer

A Brief History of His Life

  • 1930: Born in Rotterdam, Netherlands
  • 1942: Entered the Gymnasium Erasminium
  • 1945: Entered the University of Leiden, Netherlands to study theoretical physics.
  • 1951: Receives Masters in Mathematics and Physics, Leiden, NL
  • 1956: Received PhD Theoretical Physics, Leiden, NL
  • 1956: Came up with the "shortest-path algorithm"
  • 1957: Married Maria Debets
  • 1968: "GO TO statement considered harmful"
  • 1972: Received ACM Turing Award
  • 1982: Received Computer Pioneer Award, IEEE Computer Society
  • 2002: Died in Nuenen, Netherlands (aged: 72)
